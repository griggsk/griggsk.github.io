CTL Project Overview

A research center at the University of Oregon, the Center on Teaching and Learning (CTL), developed DIBELSÂ® (Dynamic Indicators of Basic Early Literacy Skills), a set of tools for assessing early literacy. The CTL and DIBELS websites are essential resources, providing access to research findings, training materials, staff profiles, and other key information. My primary role as lead designer and developer for these two content websites was to create scalable platforms that ensured the reliable delivery of this critical information.

A software migration was necessary for the CTL Drupal site to address critical security updates and provide a refreshed user experience. To align with new university branding guidelines, the migration included the implementation of the branded Drupal theme, a thorough content review, and the deployment of onboarding tools for consistent content management.

To accommodate a change in business direction, the DIBELS content was migrated from its legacy PHP platform to a Drupal CMS. This migration, featured the implementation of the university branded Drupal theme, a thorough content audit, and the creation of content editor roles.

The content migration of these two sites to Drupal ensures long-term accessibility and an enhanced user experience. Through a robust Drupal platform, educators and researchers now benefit from streamlined content access, secure downloads, and improved search functionality.

Key Deliverables

My contributions spanned the entire project lifecycle, encompassing conception, planning, and implementation.

UX/UI Design

Comprehensive content review, user needs assessment, and stakeholder feedback informed the sitemap and wireframe development for the two sites. Using university-branded design components, detailed mockups of key pages and interactive elements were then created to refine the site architecture.

Web Development

Two fully functional, university-branded Drupal web applications were deployed, providing editor accounts, secure downloads, and comprehensive research project pages. Collaborative development and streamlined environment provisioning were achieved through Git version control and Pantheon's infrastructure management. Rigorous testing, including WCAG compliance checks, validated a seamless user experience across devices and browsers. Ongoing support and maintenance ensured post-launch platform stability and user satisfaction.

Content Coordination

Working closely with researchers and subject matter experts, content and media assets were systematically gathered and managed within a collaborative project platform. Standardized templates were developed to ensure consistent data entry for research findings, training materials, and staff profiles. Content underwent a comprehensive review process, with multiple rounds of peer feedback, to ensure accuracy and clarity.
