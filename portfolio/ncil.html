    NCIL Project Overview

    The National Center on Improving Literacy (NCIL), with funding from the United States Department of Education, is a partnership among literacy experts, university researchers, and technical assistance providers. NCIL mision is to increase access to, and use of, evidence-based approaches to screen, identify, and teach students with literacy-related disabilities, including dyslexia.

    As the lead designer and developer, my primary role was to create a scalable platform for disseminating critical literacy resources and tools. I collaborated extensively with researchers to understand their needs and vision, translating their expertise into a user-friendly and adaptable website. The site, designed to grow alongside evolving research and content, featured audience-specific pages, a comprehensive resource library, engaging infographics, educational videos, instructional guides, and interactive tools. Leveraging Drupal's content management capabilities, I implemented the site, ensuring a robust and maintainable platform for the dissemination of vital literacy resources.

    Following the seven-year grant period, the Drupal site underwent a necessary software update and server migration. A user-centered redesign was driven by research, including website analytics, content review, and card sorting, which led to the development of an optimized sitemap. I then executed the migration of the 1,000+ page Drupal 7 website to Drupal 9 and deployed it on the Pantheon platform.

    In 2024, the grant was transferred to another university, and site is now handled through a different approach.

    Key Deliverables

    My contributions spanned the entire project lifecycle, encompassing conception, planning, and implementation.

    UX Research

    Through user-centered research, including card sorting, analytics review, and competitive analysis, NCIL's audience and content needs were identified. This informed the creation of strategic content models, streamlined user flows, and detailed wireframes. User feedback refined the site's information architecture, culminating in the development of mockups and a content inventory that ensured a smooth sitemap transition.

    UI Design

    The UI design aimed to create an intuitive and accessible user experience, with a focus on creating a design system that could scale with future product releases. A comprehensive design system, including detailed style guides and component libraries, was developed, and a process was implemented to design new product components that seamlessly integrated with the existing framework. High-fidelity mockups were refined through iterative user feedback, ensuring alignment with both current and future product designs.

    Web Development

    A fully functional Drupal web application was developed from the UI/UX designs, leveraging the Bootstrap design library for a responsive and modern interface. Custom content editor tools were created to empower content managers, enabling efficient content updates. Hosted on Pantheon, standard DevOps practices streamlined development and deployment. Comprehensive testing, including WCAG accessibility checks, validated functionality and performance across devices and browsers. Support and maintenance ensured platform stability and user satisfaction.

    Content Coordination

    Utilizing an agile approach with iterative development and continuous collaboration, requirements were managed in a shared project management tool. Stakeholder collaboration ensured systematic collection and organization of content and media. Standardized templates facilitated consistent data capture, and product copy was refined through peer reviews. Agile methodologies continued post-launch with bug ticket management, sprint planning, and feature enhancements.
