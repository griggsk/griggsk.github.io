CTL Marketplace Project Overview

The CTL Marketplace provides a selection of evidence-based, multi-tiered mathematics and literacy products designed for students in grades K-8. My primary responsibility as lead designer and developer was to design, develop, and deploy a modern, user-friendly, and efficient e-commerce platform, ensuring seamless transactions and an intuitive user interface.

CTL's former sales process, which relied on a complex PDF form and a disconnected university credit card payment website, proved inadequate for handling increased sales volume. This resulted in a poor customer experience and left administrators without the necessary reporting and reconciliation capabilities for credit card transactions. To address these issues, I implemented a Drupal e-commerce site with a custom payment gateway, customer accounts, and robust administrative reporting. This new system offers streamlined purchasing for customers and comprehensive order management and insightful sales analytics for administrators.

Key Deliverables

My contributions spanned the entire project lifecycle, encompassing conception, planning, and implementation.

UI Design

Guided by industry best practices for e-commerce design, the UI development prioritized an intuitive and accessible user experience. Refining the site architecture through detailed mockups of key pages and interactive elements resulted in a streamlined checkout, comprehensive product pages, and automated email receipts.

Web Development

The UI designs were translated into a robust Drupal Commerce web application, with customer accounts, comprehensive administrative tools, and detailed product pages. A custom module enabled secure payment processing through the university's credit card API. Git and Pantheon's infrastructure management tools facilitated collaborative development and streamlined environment provisioning. Rigorous testing, including accessibility compliance, ensured cross-device and browser functionality. Post-launch support and maintenance guaranteed platform stability and user satisfaction.

Content Coordination

Through active collaboration with stakeholders, product content and media were systematically collected and organized using a collaborative project management tool. Consistent data capture was achieved through the development of standardized templates, and product copy was refined through multiple rounds of peer review.
